MDS Biplot

final = data.frame(m_ordinal$conf)
final_bi = biplotmds(m_ordinal, 
                     extvar = newdata[1:7])$coefficients 
ggplot() + geom_point(data = final, aes(x=D1,y=D2)) +
  
  #  Country points
  geom_text_repel(data = final, 
                  aes(x = D1, y = D2, label=row.names(final)),
                  size = 2, min.segment.length = 0) +
  
  # Emission Type vectors
  geom_segment(data = as.data.frame(t(final_bi)),
               aes(x=0,y=0,xend=D1,yend=D2),alpha=0.3,
               arrow = arrow(length = unit(0.2, "cm"), type = "closed")) +
  
  # Emission Type coord end
  geom_text(data = as.data.frame(t(final_bi)), col = "brown",
                  aes(x = D1, y = D2, label=colnames(final_bi)),
                  size = 2.5) +
  
  labs(title="Country Emissions Similarities 2021💀",x="",y="")

Bright Side: Good News! Most of the countries are clustered at the right side of the biplot - which suggests that these countries do not have much emission of any kind. Brazil, Malta and Sweden have the least emmission volume among other countries. Liechtenstein, Latvia, Portugal, Switzerland, Romania and some other countries are so similar in terms of emissions, so they label cannot be seen on the graph (see plot below). Then we have some european (at least in terms of continent) countries - Ukraine, Turkey, Slovenia, Bulgaria, Slovakia, Finland and Austria also with low values of emissions.

Somewhere in the middle: Closer to zero coordinates, we can observe two groups of countries that already have a greater negative impact on the environment. The first group includes Ireland, Norway, Belgium and Netherlands: those countries mostly have oil and gas emissions, they are not really high, but we can see the difference of these countries from those countries which are on the “Bright Side”. The second group is about Coal, Cement and “other” emissions: Germany, Japan, Estonia, Poland, Czech Republic. These are industrial countries and they produce significant volume of emissions, especially china.

💀: But of course, there are always would be the bad guys. On the left side of the graph you can see highly heterogeous countries with high volumes of emissions. They are not similar, every country has its own specialization. Gas and Oil Emissions mostly produced by USA and Canada (sorry, Russia was an outlier), Australia and Kazakhstan are on fire but they really like to throw only more coal into the furnace - High Coal and Flaring emissions, Taiwan and South Korea are just burning - mostly have only Flaring Emissions.
